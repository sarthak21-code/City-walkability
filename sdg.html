Understanding SDG 12

Why responsible consumption matters, and how walkable cities help achieve it.

SDG 12: Responsible Consumption and Production

SDG 12.5: Substantially Reduce Waste Generation

What is SDG 12?

SDG 12 stands for Responsible Consumption and Production. It focuses on using resources wisely, reducing waste, and making sure what we consume does not harm the planet.

Today, cities generate massive amounts of waste due to inefficient consumption patterns. SDG 12 encourages people, industries, and governments to shift towards sustainable lifestyles.

Why SDG 12 Matters

  • Reduces pollution and landfill waste
  • Promotes eco-friendly practices in daily life
  • Encourages smarter city planning
  • Helps conserve natural resources
  • Improves long-term quality of life

What is SDG 12.5?

SDG 12.5 specifically aims to substantially reduce waste generation by 2030 through:

  • Recycling
  • Reusing
  • Reducing single-use materials
  • Improving waste collection systems

How Walkability Supports SDG 12 & 12.5

A walkable city encourages sustainable behavior naturally. Here's how:

  • Less dependency on vehicles → reduced fuel waste
  • More public infrastructure like dustbins → reduced littering
  • Cleaner footpaths → organized waste management
  • More drinking water refill points → lower plastic bottle usage

Walkable cities don’t just look better — they consume less, waste less, and support a greener future.
